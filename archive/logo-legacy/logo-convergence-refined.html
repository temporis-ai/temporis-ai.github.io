Temporis Convergence Refinements

These refinements reduce the original convergence idea until the silhouette carries more of the meaning. The question here is simple: how little structure can we keep while still reading as multiple temporal paths resolving into one useful signal?

Refinement A

Dual Merge

Two strands merge into one. This is the simplest legible convergence version and the best candidate for small-size performance.

This is the cleanest convergence idea: one primary trunk, one secondary signal feeding in. Strongest reduction of the group.

Best practical candidate. Strong concept with much less complexity.

Refinement B

Triple Merge

Keeps three strands, but simplifies them into thicker, shorter paths so the symbol feels less like a diagram and more like a mark.

More expressive than the dual version, but still much simpler than the original convergence drawing.

Best if you want to preserve the richness of the original convergence idea.

Refinement C

Open Convergence

The merged output is open-ended rather than enclosed. This gives the mark a lighter, more forward-looking feeling.

This version feels more open and less terminal. It may fit a company brand better than a fully contained output form.

Best if the mark should feel less “sealed” and more alive. Risk: less self-contained as an icon.
