Temporis: Making Time Readable

This round starts from the company idea rather than from time motifs. If Temporis is about making time readable, then the mark should feel like ordering, resolving, clarifying, or indexing time. The symbol should not “look like time.” It should look like clarity applied to time.

Brand center: Temporis makes time readable.

Implication for form: the logo should suggest ordering, resolution, framing, indexing, or clarified signal.

What to avoid: literal clocks, timeline branches, decorative futurism, mini dashboards.

Direction P

Resolver

A broad uncertain field compressed into one clear terminal line. This is about resolution: many possibilities becoming one readable output.

Most directly tied to the brand claim. The mark is about reducing ambiguity into a usable line.

Strength: best direct link to the company. Risk: still slightly complex in the center turn.

Direction Q

Indexer

A stable spine with two measured indicators. This is less about “time passing” and more about making time legible and indexed.

Clean and editorial. The brand idea here is temporal indexing rather than prediction or motion.

Strength: very clear and calm. Risk: may be too generic unless the proportions become distinctive.

Direction R

Clarified Signal

A broad band resolves into one narrow calibrated line. This is the simplest form of “making noisy time into readable signal.”

This may be the cleanest brand-to-form translation in the set. It is about signal becoming legible, not time being illustrated.

Strength: strongest connection to the company claim. Risk: still a touch intricate in the turn.
