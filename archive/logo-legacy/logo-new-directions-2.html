Temporis New Directions 2

This round explores additional abstract ideas for time that stay within the same standards: low complexity, strong silhouette, and conceptual defensibility. These are not chart icons. They are attempts to encode cadence, interval, recurrence, and temporal shift in more ownable forms.

Direction L

Interval Gate

A portal-like form cut by one measured interval. This uses “time window” as a stronger silhouette rather than as bars inside a box.

More architectural than the previous box family. The difference is that the outer silhouette is now the idea, not just a neutral container.

Strength: stronger outer identity. Risk: may still feel a bit like a UI panel.

Direction M

Pulse Arc

A curved sweep interrupted by one measured pulse. This tries to combine elegance with a clear timing event.

Cleaner than a full arc family and easier to scale. The pulse gives the sweep a timing cue instead of making it purely decorative.

Strength: elegant and compact. Risk: the pulse may be too subtle to matter enough.

Direction N

Marker Pair

Two vertical markers at different states. This is a much more editorial reading of time series as comparison between moments.

Very simple, almost editorial. It may be a useful alternative if all of the more symbolic directions feel overworked.

Strength: brutally simple. Risk: maybe too generic and too close to generic analytics symbols.

Direction O

Folded Interval

One solid interval appears folded across time. This is a more singular, object-like way to show temporal transformation.

More abstract and more object-like than the others. This could become a stronger logo if it avoids feeling like a generic folded shape.

Strength: more object-like and ownable. Risk: weaker direct tie to time series unless framed well.
