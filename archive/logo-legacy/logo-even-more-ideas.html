Temporis Even More Ideas

This round looks for stronger identity moves without abandoning the time-series domain. The marks here try different primitives: nesting, cadence, offset, and accumulation. The goal is still the same: simple, small-scale readable, and defensible without turning into product UI.

Direction H

Accumulation Mark

Three short blocks gather into one stronger mass. Time series is implied as accumulation across intervals instead of linear plotting.

Very direct sequence logic, but with a stronger diagonal accumulation feel than a conventional chart. Could become a memorable shorthand if proportioned well.

Strength: immediate progression. Risk: may still read a bit like a stylized mini chart.

Direction I

Nested Frame

A larger interval partially encloses a smaller one. The mark suggests windows within windows, which is a strong metaphor for time-series context.

More system-like than the V1 family, but still simple. It could work if Temporis wants the logo to hint at layered analysis rather than pure progression.

Strength: layered meaning without much complexity. Risk: less distinctive silhouette.

Direction J

Arc Pair

Two measured arcs imply passage through time instead of stacked intervals. This is a softer, more continuous reading of time series.

More elegant and less blocky than most of the bar-based ideas. If it works, it could feel more like a true brand mark than a data abstraction.

Strength: could feel more premium and less literal. Risk: small-size read depends heavily on curvature.

Direction K

Measured Split

A strong horizontal mass with one smaller elevated interval. This uses contrast in duration and placement as the identity.

This is closer to a symbol of measured intervals than a picture of time series. It is bold, reduced, and maybe easier to remember than the boxed variants.

Strength: bolder and more logo-like. Risk: weaker immediate “time series” read.
