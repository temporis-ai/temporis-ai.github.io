Temporis Dual Merge Push

`Refinement A` is one of the better concept-to-logo bridges so far: strong conceptual fit, better distinctiveness than the box-and-bar family, and still simple enough to reduce. Its weakness is that the current drawing is slightly too intricate in the merge area. This round simplifies and strengthens the geometry.

Evaluation of A: strong on distinctiveness and brand fit, moderate on simplicity, good but not yet excellent on small-size behavior.

What to improve: reduce bends, thicken the main path, make the merge more immediate, and give the symbol a clearer primary silhouette.

What to keep: dual-path convergence, abstract time logic, and a non-UI feeling.

Push 1

Cleaner Merge

The secondary path is shortened and the main trunk is thickened. This makes the symbol read more like one mark instead of two joined paths.

Strongest all-around refinement. The convergence idea is still there, but the mark is quieter and more self-contained.

Most likely improvement path if this direction stays alive.

Push 2

Heavier Trunk

The merged output becomes more dominant and the secondary signal becomes shorter. This improves hierarchy and small-size performance.

More decisive and practical. Less nuanced than Push 1, but stronger when reduced.

Best if favicon and app-icon performance matter most.

Push 3

Open Merge

The merge remains visible, but the output side is more open and less block-like. This makes the mark feel lighter and more elegant.

Most elegant and least blocky. It feels more like a brand gesture, but also slightly less authoritative than the stronger trunk versions.

Best if elegance matters more than maximum solidity.
