Temporis More Ideas

This round explores new visual grammars while keeping the same principles: one strong move, small-size clarity, and a defensible link to time series. None of these are mini dashboards or boxed charts. Each is trying to create recognition through a different kind of cadence.

Direction D

Stepped Pair

Two intervals become two stacked steps with a shared corner. It is still a pair, but now the silhouette itself carries progression.

Progression is built into the outer form. This could be more ownable than bars, but still calm enough for a technical brand.

Potentially more memorable than the current V1 family. Risk: can feel a bit assertive.

Direction E

Nested Shift

A larger outer interval contains a smaller shifted one. Time series becomes nested change rather than two separate bars.

Less like two independent marks and more like one system with internal movement. It may be a better metaphor for series within series.

Calmer than the stepped approach. Risk: may not separate enough from generic signal graphics.

Direction F

Pulse Block

A single block interrupted by one shorter block. This treats time series as a cadence of measured durations instead of lines or steps.

Very reduced, very strong at small sizes. The brand cue comes from duration contrast rather than from framing or stacked sequence.

Strong reduction and good recall potential. Risk: the link to time series is less obvious, more interpretive.

Direction G

Phase Mark

Two horizontal intervals plus one vertical phase shift. This tries to represent time series as change in state rather than just length difference.

This is the most unusual idea in the set. It uses a phase change instead of simple parallel comparison, which may create a stronger memory hook.

Could become the most ownable. Risk: it is also the least immediately obvious.
