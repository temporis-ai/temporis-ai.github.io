Temporis Time Concept Directions

These directions translate time ideas like loom, branching, slipping, and convergence into abstract marks. The goal is not to borrow `Loki` aesthetics. It is to borrow useful concepts: time as woven structure, path divergence, recurring identity across moments, and ordered convergence into one readable form.

Concept 51

Convergence

Three strands compress into one clean channel. This is the strongest direct metaphor for forecasting: many temporal possibilities, one resolved signal.

Best conceptual fit. If simplified well, this could become a genuinely ownable signal-to-forecast mark.

Strength: strongest story-to-symbol translation. Risk: may still be too complex if not reduced further.

Concept 52

Time Slip

One shape repeated with a slight displacement. This suggests the same signal appearing across adjacent moments.

The easiest of the set to understand. It is simple, sequence-led, and visually tied to time displacement rather than static comparison.

Strength: clear concept and strong rhythm. Risk: may feel too much like layered UI states unless refined further.

Concept 53

Branch Loom

One stable trunk with two controlled branches. It combines time branching with ordered structure instead of chaotic divergence.

More structural than the others. This could be useful if the brand should imply control over branching possibilities rather than only continuity.

Strength: strong symbolic logic. Risk: could drift toward generic analytics or routing marks.

Concept 54

Temporal Loom

Three bands weave toward a tighter center. This is the most direct translation of the loom idea into a compact abstract mark.

The richest concept in the set. If simplified correctly, it could become very specific to Temporis. Right now it is the most conceptually loaded.

Strength: deepest conceptual fit. Risk: easiest to become too complex for a logo.
