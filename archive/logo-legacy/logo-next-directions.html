Temporis Next Directions

`V1` is strong because it is simple, calm, and readable when small. What it is still missing is a stronger memory cue. The next step is not adding more detail. It is finding one clearer visual move that can make the mark feel more like Temporis and less like a generic system icon.

What is strong in V1: simple two-interval logic, good small-size behavior, stable container, low gimmick risk.

What is missing: a more ownable silhouette or cadence, stronger recall, less “template icon” feeling.

What should stay constant: simplicity, calm tone, time-series adjacency, no dashboard look, no lettermarks, no extra explanation.

Direction A

Interval Kink

Keep the basic container idea, but break the lower interval into a subtle bend. It still reads as a measured interval, but gains a more ownable cadence.

The best case for this direction is that it gives the mark a more specific internal rhythm without making it busier or more literal.

Potentially more ownable. Risk: the “kink” may feel overly clever if pushed too far.

Direction B

Offset Pair

Drop the container completely and let the two intervals become the identity. The mark becomes a pure cadence symbol rather than a framed icon.

This is the purest version of the current logic. If it works, it would be more logo-like and less icon-template-like than the boxed versions.

Potentially the cleanest long-term brand move. Risk: may become too minimal unless the cadence feels unmistakable.

Direction C

Column Pair

Rotate the idea ninety degrees. Instead of intervals laid across time, the mark becomes a pair of measured columns with different heights.

This keeps the idea of measured comparison, but turns it into a more monument-like symbol. It is simpler and stronger in silhouette than the bar-in-box family.

Potentially strongest silhouette. Risk: may drift toward generic “analytics” unless the proportions are tuned carefully.
