Temporis Arc Spectrum Direction

This direction explores a restrained “rainbow-like” idea: nested arcs rather than stacked bars. The goal is to imply continuity, ordered progression, and temporal sweep without becoming colorful, decorative, or soft. These are still technical symbols, just with a more continuous grammar.

Arc 1

Nested Sweep

Three nested arcs with one dominant outer sweep. This is the clearest “rainbow-like” interpretation while staying controlled.

Strongest continuity read. It feels more like a true brand mark than the bar family, but still carries a clear sense of ordered progression.

Best balance of elegance and clarity. Risk: slightly softer brand feel than a more block-based mark.

Arc 2

Broken Spectrum

Nested arcs with one intentional gap. The break creates a stronger memory cue and avoids a purely decorative rainbow read.

The missing middle creates a more ownable silhouette. This is less smooth, but potentially more distinctive and less “pretty for its own sake.”

Best if distinctiveness matters more than a fully continuous arc system.

Arc 3

Compact Arc Block

The arcs are shorter and thicker, so the mark feels more compact and robust. This is the most practical version for small-scale use.

Less delicate, more compact, and easier to reproduce small. It trades some elegance for practical strength.

Best if the arc direction should remain viable in very small product contexts.
