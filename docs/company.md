## Why time series matters
Time series is the structure behind data that changes over time.

Examples include:

- prices
- volatility
- volume
- sensor readings
- business metrics

Temporis uses time series as the technical frame because many real decisions depend on understanding how signals change, interact, and evolve.

For non-technical readers, this can be expressed more simply as:

- information that changes over time
- patterns in prices, demand, or other changing signals
- decisions about what may happen next

## Why financial markets first
Financial markets are the current starting point because they force rigor.

They also make forecasting easy to demonstrate because the domain is familiar to many people and the results are visible quickly.

They make the following things visible very quickly:

- timing quality
- signal quality
- noise tolerance
- communication discipline
- honest handling of uncertainty

This makes financial forecasting a demanding starting point for building both model quality and product quality.
