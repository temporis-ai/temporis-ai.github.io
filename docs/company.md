# Temporis Company Source

## Purpose
This document is the source of truth for company content used across the public website.

It defines:

- what Temporis is
- what the company is building
- how the company should be described
- what factual claims are safe to make
- what themes the homepage and other public pages can draw from

This document is not a layout or page-structure brief. That belongs in [requirements.md](requirements.md).

## Working model
This document has two layers:

- refined source content for page writing
- raw founder answers captured during working sessions

The raw-answer section is intentionally less polished. It exists to preserve meaning before it is translated into public-facing language.

When there is tension between the two:

- treat the raw answers as founder intent
- treat the refined sections as audience-adapted website content

## Why time series matters
Time series is the structure behind data that changes over time.

Examples include:

- prices
- volatility
- volume
- sensor readings
- business metrics

Temporis uses time series as the technical frame because many real decisions depend on understanding how signals change, interact, and evolve.

For non-technical readers, this can be expressed more simply as:

- information that changes over time
- patterns in prices, demand, or other changing signals
- decisions about what may happen next

## Why financial markets first
Financial markets are the first domain because they force rigor.

They also make forecasting easy to demonstrate because the domain is familiar to many people and the results are visible quickly.

They make the following things visible very quickly:

- timing quality
- signal quality
- noise tolerance
- communication discipline
- honest handling of uncertainty

This makes financial forecasting a demanding starting point for building both model quality and product quality.
