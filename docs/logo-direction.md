# Temporis Logo Direction

## Purpose
This document turns the existing brand and website docs into a practical logo recommendation for Temporis.

It is company-specific, unlike the generic process guidance in [logo.md](../logo.md).

## Brand Signals From The Existing Docs

Across [requirements.md](/Users/yijiang/Documents/temporis-ai.github.io/docs/requirements.md), [design.md](/Users/yijiang/Documents/temporis-ai.github.io/docs/design.md), and [value-proposition.md](/Users/yijiang/Documents/temporis-ai.github.io/docs/value-proposition.md), the brand is consistently defined as:

- precise
- calm
- technical
- rigorous
- trustworthy
- modern
- restrained

The logo should therefore feel:

- more like forecasting infrastructure than a trading product
- more like a research platform than a consumer app
- more like a durable system than a flashy AI brand

The docs also rule out several obvious dead ends:

- no clocks
- no stopwatch or literal time icons
- no finance-arrow cliches
- no crypto-neon style
- no mascots
- no decorative futurism

## Recommendation

The right direction is:

1. a custom **`Temporis` wordmark**
2. a distinct **`TS` logo mark**

These should be designed as one system, not as a primary logo plus an afterthought.

Reason:

- `Temporis` is a distinctive name and should do branding work directly
- `TS` is strategically strong because it means both `Temporis` and `time series`
- a raw `TS` monogram is still easy to make generic, finance-like, or enterprise-software-like unless it is drawn with discipline
- the system needs both long-form brand recognition and compact recognition

## Anthropic Reference

Anthropic is a useful reference for practice, not for imitation.

Based on Anthropic's current public site at `anthropic.com`, the company-level identity appears to rely on:

- a clean wordmark rather than a symbol-led identity
- restrained typography
- high confidence in spacing, scale, and composition
- almost no decorative signaling inside the logo itself

That is a strong fit for Temporis because the surrounding docs already call for:

- a calm brand
- a technically serious tone
- low hype
- trust through restraint

What should transfer from Anthropic:

- build both a company wordmark and a compact company mark
- let typography carry authority
- avoid trying to encode the full company story inside one complex symbol
- let the wordmark and compact mark carry different jobs inside one identity system
- use the logo as an anchor, then let the website and product visuals carry more of the narrative

What should not transfer blindly:

- copying Anthropic's exact uppercase tone
- copying their letterforms or spacing
- reducing Temporis to a generic text treatment with no owned detail

For Temporis, the lesson is:

The company identity can be simpler than the conceptual exploration work so far. It does not need to visibly illustrate forecasting in the same way product graphics do, but it should still produce both a memorable wordmark and a memorable compact mark.

## Verdict On `TS`

`TS` is a valid direction if the letters are treated as a **structured temporal form**, not as plain initials.

The strongest interpretation is:

- `T` as the stable trunk or time-axis
- `S` as an ordered signal path or stepped temporal flow

This is better than making a literal serif `T` and `S` lockup. A literal monogram would be too common and too detached from the forecasting story.

## Best Direction

### Direction A: Temporal TS
This should be the lead direction for the compact company mark.

Build a monogram where:

- the `T` is upright, stable, and simple
- the `S` is not written as a conventional letterform
- instead, the `S` is implied through two or three rounded horizontal intervals that shift around the `T`
- the overall silhouette should still read as one compact mark before it reads as two letters

What this communicates:

- time-axis plus signal
- sequence across moments
- structured movement, not volatility

What to avoid:

- sharp zig-zag strokes
- candlestick-chart energy
- italic motion marks
- obvious arrowheads

### Direction B: Wordmark With Embedded Time Logic
This should be developed in parallel with the `TS` mark and is the direction most aligned with Anthropic's practice.

Use `Temporis` as the main logo and add one quiet custom move, such as:

- a custom `T` crossbar with slightly stepped timing
- a subtle terminal treatment on `S`
- a rhythmic spacing system that suggests intervals

This keeps the company name front and center while making the wordmark feel owned.

The goal is not for the wordmark to explain forecasting on its own. The goal is for it to feel:

- exact
- credible
- memorable through proportion and letterform decisions
- naturally related to the `TS` mark

This is likely the best choice for:

- homepage header
- investor materials
- legal pages
- company-level branding

### Direction C: Existing Bar/Sequence Mark, Refined
The current mark in [logo-mark.svg](/Users/yijiang/Documents/temporis-ai.github.io/logo-mark.svg) already expresses sequence and layered signal.

Its strengths:

- calm
- simple
- works at small sizes
- aligned with the current color system

Its weakness:

- it does not feel ownable enough yet
- it reads more as a generic signal motif than as `Temporis`
- it has little connection to the name

If retained, it should be reframed as a product or system motif rather than the main `TS` company mark.

## Recommendation Ranking

1. **Matched `Temporis` wordmark + `TS` mark system**
2. **Temporal `TS` mark**
3. **Custom `Temporis` wordmark**
4. **Current stacked-sequence mark as fallback or supporting motif**

This ranking fits the existing docs better than a monogram-only system, and it aligns with the two-part company-brand practice seen in Anthropic's branding.

## Shape Guidance

If the `TS` mark is explored, use these constraints:

- rounded corners, not sharp corners
- medium stroke weight, not thin-line minimalism
- one dominant silhouette
- two-color maximum in standard use
- fully usable in one color
- no enclosed badge unless needed for app icon usage

Preferred geometry:

- vertical trunk for `T`
- short, confident top bar
- `S` formed from stepped or offset horizontal segments
- slight asymmetry is acceptable if it improves recognition

## Color Guidance

The logo must succeed in one color first.

Primary brand colors should stay aligned with the existing website direction:

- `Ink` `#0F1722`
- `Horizon Blue` `#2563EB`
- `Signal Teal` `#0F8C82`

Default recommendation:

- primary logo: one-color `Ink`
- accent version: `Ink` plus either `Horizon Blue` or `Signal Teal`
- avoid three-color marks for the main identity

Three-color usage is better for concept exploration than for the final system.

## Typography Direction

The wordmark should be:

- sans-serif
- likely uppercase or small-caps leaning in spirit, if testing supports it
- low-contrast
- modern
- not geometric to the point of feeling generic
- not quirky
- not luxury-editorial

The letterspacing should feel deliberate and slightly open, but not airy.

The main source of distinctiveness in the wordmark should come from:

- proportion
- spacing
- one or two custom letter decisions

not from adding obvious symbolism inside every character.

The ideal tone is:

- serious enough for investors
- clear enough for product UI
- modern enough for an AI-native company

## Practical System

The logo system should likely have three layers:

1. **Primary:** `Temporis` wordmark
2. **Primary compact mark:** `TS` logo mark
3. **Supporting motif:** sequence/time-window graphics used in product and website backgrounds

That separation is important. It prevents one symbol from being forced to do every job.

The first two layers should be treated as the company identity itself, not as primary and optional.

## Decision

If choosing one direction now:

- do build a **custom `Temporis` wordmark**
- do build a **temporal `TS` company mark**
- do make sure they feel related in weight, geometry, and tone
- do **not** make the `TS` a plain initials lockup

In short:

Anthropic is a good reference for logo practice because it shows the value of a disciplined company identity system. For Temporis, that should mean both a strong wordmark and a strong `TS` mark, designed together.
