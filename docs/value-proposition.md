# Temporis Value Proposition

## The trader's problem

Short-term traders need two things to make money:

1. **Strategy** — the rules and logic that govern when and how to trade. This is non-negotiable. Without a strategy, there is no systematic way to act on market conditions.

2. **Prediction** — a forward-looking view of where prices, volatility, or other market variables are heading. This is not strictly required, but it is a powerful amplifier. A good prediction sharpens entry and exit timing, improves risk sizing, and reduces the cost of being wrong.

Most traders already have a strategy, or are building one. What they often lack is a reliable, structured source of forward-looking market signals they can integrate into that strategy.

## Where Temporis fits

Temporis provides **forecasting as a service**.

The core idea: traders should not have to build and maintain their own forecasting models. Temporis does that work — training, running, and serving AI-powered time-series forecasts — so traders can focus on what they do best: designing and executing strategy.

### How this creates value

```
Trader's strategy + Temporis forecasts = better-informed decisions
```

Temporis does not replace the trader's judgment or strategy. It enhances the decision-making process by supplying a structured, model-driven view of likely future market behavior. The trader wires Temporis forecasts into their existing workflow, and uses them as one input — alongside their own analysis, risk rules, and market intuition — to make more informed trading decisions.

### The key insight

Strategy is the trader's domain. Forecasting is ours.

Traders know their markets, their risk tolerance, and their execution approach. Temporis knows how to build, validate, and serve time-series forecasts at the quality and speed that short-term trading demands. By separating these concerns, each side does what it is best at.

## Two products, one value chain

Temporis delivers forecasting through two products, each serving a different type of trader:

### Temporis Vista
For traders who want to **see** forecasts and use them visually in their decision-making.

- Chart-based forecast interface
- Designed for quick visual assessment
- Useful for discretionary traders or anyone who makes decisions by reading charts

### Temporis Platform
For traders who want to **consume** forecasts programmatically and wire them directly into automated or semi-automated strategies.

- API-based forecast access
- Structured data output for integration into trading systems, models, or dashboards
- Useful for systematic traders, quant teams, or developers building trading tools

## Summary

| | Trader's responsibility | Temporis's responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| **Strategy** | Define and execute | — |
| **Prediction** | — | Build, serve, and maintain |
| **Decision** | Make the call | Inform the call |

Temporis exists to make the prediction side of trading easier, faster, and more accessible — so traders can spend their time on strategy and execution, not on building forecasting infrastructure.
